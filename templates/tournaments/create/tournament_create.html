Rulebook

Organizer Guidelines

▸Fair play first. Ensure all participants agree to a no-cheating, no-smurfing policy before joining.
▸Clear communication. Announce schedule changes at least 24 hours in advance via community links.
▸Result submission. Require screenshot proof within 10 minutes of match end. Disputes must be filed within 15 minutes.
▸Bracket integrity. Never modify match results manually without documented reason and participant acknowledgement.
▸Prize payouts. Release prize within 72 hours of finals. Delays must be communicated publicly.
▸Disqualification. You may disqualify participants for rule violations — document and announce every DQ publicly.
▸No-show policy. Define and clearly state the waiting period (recommended: 10 min) before a no-show forfeit.

Tournament Organizer Guide

Everything you need to run a successful event

Wizard Steps

Game → Details → Format → Schedule & Payment → Rules → Review → Launch

1 — Before You Create

Choose the right game. Pick the game your community plays most. All format defaults and rules will be pre-tuned for it.

Decide your format first. Single Elimination is easiest to run. Double Elimination gives players a second chance. Round Robin suits smaller groups.

Set realistic dates. Allow at least 5–7 days for registration before the event starts. Check-in should open 15–30 min before match time.

2 — Participant Management

Use check-in. Enable check-in 15–30 minutes before start. Participants who miss check-in can be replaced with reserves.

Cap your capacity smartly. A power-of-2 bracket (8, 16, 32, 64) avoids byes and is easier to manage. Use Round Robin for odd numbers.

Verify payment manually if needed. For Bkash/Nagad payments, cross-check transaction IDs before approving slots. Use the TOC participant panel.

3 — Running the Event

Start matches on time. Delays cascade. If a team is late, use a 10-minute no-show rule and move on. Announce this in your rulebook.

Require screenshot proof. All winners must submit match result screenshots within 10 minutes. No proof = no confirmed result.

Handle disputes quickly. Only accept disputes filed within 15 minutes of a result. Document every ruling publicly in your community channel.

Never edit brackets manually without reason. If you must override a result, log it clearly with screenshots and inform both sides.

4 — Prizes & Payouts

Never promise what you can't deliver. Set a prize pool you can 100% pay out before the event starts, not after.

Payout within 72 hours. Winners expect fast rewards. Delays harm your reputation as an organizer.

Use TOC for complex prizes. Multi-tier distributions, DeltaCoin payouts, and conditional bonuses are all manageable from the Tournament Operations Center after creation.

5 — After Creation

TOC Onboarding. You'll be redirected to the Tournament Operations Center with a step-by-step onboarding guide. Complete it to unlock all management tools.

Publish immediately. Share your /t/ tournament link and community channels as soon as registration opens.

Communicate proactively. Post updates on schedule changes at least 24 hours in advance. Silence from organizers kills participation.

Pro Tip

Run a test tournament with friends first. Use it to find gaps in your rulebook, test payment flow, and practice the TOC tools before going public.
