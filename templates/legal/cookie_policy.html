Understanding Cookies

DeltaCrown uses cookies and similar tracking technologies to provide, improve, and secure our esports tournament platform. This Cookie Policy explains what cookies are, how we use them, and how you can control your cookie preferences.

By using DeltaCrown, you consent to the use of cookies as described in this policy. You can manage your cookie settings at any time through your browser or our preference center.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) when you visit a website. They help websites remember your preferences, authenticate your identity, and analyze how you use the site.

First-Party Cookies

Set directly by DeltaCrown on your device. These cookies enable core functionality like authentication, session management, and user preferences.

Third-Party Cookies

Set by external services integrated into DeltaCrown (like analytics providers). These help us understand platform usage and improve performance.

Cookie Duration Types

Session Cookies: Temporary cookies deleted when you close your browser. Used for authentication and navigation.
Persistent Cookies: Remain on your device for a set period (days to years). Used for preferences and analytics.

Impact of Disabling Cookies

Blocking or deleting cookies may affect your experience on DeltaCrown:

  • • You may be logged out and unable to stay authenticated
  • • Your preferences (language, timezone, theme) will not be saved
  • • Some features may not function properly
  • • We won't be able to remember your cookie consent choices

5. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers offer a "Do Not Track" (DNT) signal that requests websites not to track your browsing activity. Currently, there is no universal standard for how websites should respond to DNT signals.

DeltaCrown honors Do Not Track signals where technically feasible. When DNT is enabled, we will not use analytics or optional tracking cookies. However, essential cookies required for authentication and security will still function.
