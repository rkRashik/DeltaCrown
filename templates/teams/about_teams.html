Terms You Should Know

Understanding esports terminology helps you navigate the competitive scene better.

Roster
Your complete team lineup including all players, substitutes, coach, and staff. Think of it as your official squad list.
Substitute (Sub)
Backup players who can replace starters. Most tournaments require at least 1-2 subs registered.
Roster Lock
Period when you cannot change your lineup, typically during active tournaments. Protects competitive integrity.
Seeding
Tournament bracket placement based on team ranking. Higher-ranked teams get better starting positions.
Fair Play Rules
Code of conduct for competitive integrity: no cheating, smurfing, collusion, or throwing matches.
Game ID
Your in-game username or identifier (Riot ID, Steam ID, etc.) required for tournament participation.
Check-In
Pre-match confirmation (usually 15-30 min before) proving your team is ready to play. Miss it = disqualification.
Verified Team
Official badge showing your team is legitimate, follows rules, and has tournament history.
